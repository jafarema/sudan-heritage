The Story
Sudan is the land where civilization was not borrowed — it was born.

Long before the great empires of Rome or Greece, the Nile Valley of Sudan was the cradle of one of humanity's most extraordinary civilizations. The Kingdom of Kerma, the Black Pharaohs of the 25th Dynasty, the pyramids of Meroe — these are not footnotes to history. They are its very foundation.

Sudan possesses more ancient pyramids than Egypt — 255 versus Egypt's 138 — yet remains one of the least explored civilizations on Earth.

The Meroitic script, developed around 300 BC, remains one of the last undeciphered writing systems of the ancient world.

Nubian queens — the Kandakes — commanded armies and ruled empires at a time when most of the world had no concept of female leadership.
